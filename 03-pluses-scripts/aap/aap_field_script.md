# Adaptation Aspirations Planning — Field Script

**Program**: QCF Uganda Cash for Climate Adaptation
**Sub-arm**: 2b
**Duration**: ~10 minutes
**Delivery**: One-on-one, in person, by field officer
**Timing**: After token ($50) confirmed, before lump sum ($594)
**Language**: Deliver in recipient's preferred language (translate from this English script)

---

## Field Officer Instructions

### Purpose
You are visiting this household to help the recipient think about how they could use their upcoming cash transfer to prepare for climate risks. You will guide them through a short planning exercise. You are a facilitator — you ask questions and listen. You do not give advice, suggest answers, or tell them what to do with their money.

### Before the visit
- Confirm the recipient received the $50 token transfer
- Bring this script, a pen, and a blank Planning Sheet for the recipient to keep
- Review the five steps below so you can guide the conversation naturally

### During the visit
- Read the prompts as written, in the recipient's preferred language
- Give the recipient time to think and respond — do not rush them
- Record their answers in the data fields marked [RECORD]
- If they struggle to answer, you may repeat or rephrase the question, but do not suggest specific answers
- If they mention a non-climate goal (e.g., school fees), acknowledge it, then gently redirect: "That is a good goal. For this exercise, let us focus on things that help your household deal with climate risks like [floods/drought]."

### After the visit
- Write the recipient's goal and first step on the Planning Sheet and give it to them
- Complete the visit log (date, recipient ID, field officer ID, completion yes/no)

### Do NOT
- Tell the recipient which group they are in or that other households are doing different exercises
- Suggest specific investments, crops, or strategies
- Promise any additional support beyond the cash transfer
- Encourage the recipient to "dream big" or "think rich" — the goal of this exercise is to help them make a realistic, concrete plan, not to set ambitious targets. If their goal seems out of reach, help them find a smaller, achievable first step

---

## Planning Exercise Script

**[Introduce yourself and the exercise]**

> "Hello, my name is [name]. I am from GiveDirectly. Thank you for your time today. You recently received a small transfer, and a larger transfer is coming soon. I would like to do a short exercise with you — about 10 minutes — to help you think about how you might prepare your household for climate risks. There are no right or wrong answers. I just want to hear your thoughts."

---

### Step 1: Climate Best Possible Self (~2 min)

> "I would like you to imagine your household two years from now. Imagine that you have used your resources well and your household is as prepared as possible for climate risks — things like [floods / drought / heavy rains / dry spells]. Your crops, your animals, your home, your income — everything is as protected as it can be. What does that look like? Take a moment to think, and then describe it to me."

**[RECORD: What the recipient describes — in their own words, as specifically as possible]**

_If they give a short or vague answer, probe:_
- "What would your farm or livelihood look like?"
- "What about your home or your stored food?"
- "Would you be earning income in a different way?"

---

### Step 2: Goal Selection (~2 min)

> "You mentioned several things: [repeat back what they said — e.g., 'a stronger roof, planting sorghum instead of maize, and starting a small shop']. These are good ideas. Now, if you could only focus on one of these — the one that is most important to you, and that you believe you could realistically achieve with the resources you have — which one would it be?"

_If they pick something very ambitious (e.g., "buy a truck," "build a new house"), gently probe:_
- "That is a good goal. Do you think you could achieve it with your transfer and what you already have? If not, is there a smaller step toward it that you could start with?"

**[RECORD: The one goal they choose]**

> "How long do you think it would take to achieve this, starting from when you receive your transfer?"

**[RECORD: Their time estimate]**

---

### Step 3: First Step (~2 min)

> "Now let us make a plan. When you receive your larger transfer, what is the very first thing you would do — this week or the week after — to start working toward [their goal]? Be as specific as you can. For example: who would you talk to, what would you buy, where would you go?"

**[RECORD: The specific first step they describe]**

---

### Step 4: Obstacles (~2 min)

> "Sometimes things do not go as planned. What is the biggest thing that could get in the way of achieving your goal of [their goal]?"

**[RECORD: The obstacle they identify]**

> "If that happens, what could you do instead? What is your backup plan?"

**[RECORD: Their backup strategy]**

---

### Step 5: Motivation (~1 min)

> "Last question. If you succeed — if you achieve your goal of [their goal] — how would your life be different from how it is now? How would it make you feel?"

**[RECORD: Their response]**

---

**[Close the exercise]**

> "Thank you. Let me write down your goal and your first step on this sheet so you can keep it."

**[Write on the Planning Sheet:]**
- My goal: [their goal]
- My first step: [their first step]

> "This is yours to keep. Where in your home could you put it so you will see it often — on a wall, near your bed, inside a cupboard you open every day? [Wait for answer.] Good. Please put it there when you go inside. Thank you for your time today, and good luck."

## Data to Record Per Visit

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| recipient_id | Household/recipient identifier |
| field_officer_id | Field officer identifier |
| visit_date | Date of visit |
| visit_completed | Yes / No / Partial |
| step1_description | What they described as their climate-prepared household (verbatim) |
| step2_goal | The one goal they selected |
| step2_timeline | Their time estimate to achieve it |
| step3_first_step | The specific first action they would take |
| step4_obstacle | The biggest obstacle they identified |
| step4_backup | Their backup plan |
| step5_motivation | How their life would be different / how it would feel |
| notes | Any other observations (e.g., recipient seemed confused, needed translation help) |
