## Design decisions (April 30, 2026)

1. **Climate-specific** — exercise framed around climate adaptation, not general aspirations. Differentiates 2b from what cash alone does (Orkin et al. show cash raises general aspirations on its own)
2. **Mentions cash transfer** — script explicitly references the upcoming $594 as a resource for planning
3. **Written plan** — recipient keeps a Planning Sheet with their goal and first step written by the FO
4. **FO records answers** — all 5 steps recorded verbatim (vision, goal, first step, obstacle, backup, motivation) for research data
5. **10 minutes** — 5 steps at ~2 min each. Compressed from Orkin et al.'s 80-min workshop
6. **No vignette/video** — purely self-directed, FO is facilitator only
7. **Plain English** — FO translates on the fly to recipient's preferred language
8. **Feasibility check** — Step 2 includes explicit probe against over-ambitious goals (informed by McKenzie et al. 2021 frustration finding)
9. **Anti-"dream big" instruction** — FO Do-NOT section explicitly warns against encouraging big dreams
10. **Commitment prompt** — closing asks recipient to name a specific location for the Planning Sheet

## Script structure (5 steps)

| Step | Name | Duration | Prompt (summary) | Based on |
|------|------|----------|-------------------|----------|
| 1 | Climate Best Possible Self | ~2 min | "Imagine your household 2 years from now, prepared for climate risks. What does that look like?" | Orkin: Best Possible Selves (King 2001) |
| 2 | Goal Selection | ~2 min | "Which one goal is most important AND realistic?" + feasibility check | Orkin: Personal Goal-Setting (Morisano et al. 2010) |
| 3 | First Step | ~2 min | "What is the very first thing you would do this week?" | Orkin: Implementation Intentions (Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006) |
| 4 | Obstacles | ~2 min | "What could go wrong? What is your backup?" | Orkin: Anticipation of Obstacles (Duckworth et al. 2013) |
| 5 | Motivation | ~1 min | "How would life be different if you succeed?" | Orkin: Mental Contrasting (Oettingen & Gollwitzer 2010) |

**Omitted from Orkin et al.**: Role model videos (no video capacity), drawing exercise (time constraint), group sharing (individual visit), reminder calendar (replaced with Planning Sheet).

## Key literature

### Orkin et al. (2023) — "Aspiring to a Better Future" (Kenya, with GiveDirectly)
- 80-min workshop: role model videos + best possible selves + goal-setting + implementation intentions + reminder calendar
- 415 villages, 8,300 people, 17-month follow-up
- Workshop alone: +22% productive inputs, +11% revenue, +4% consumption, +6% assets
- **Workshop + cash ≈ cash alone** — cash itself raised aspirations, crowding out the workshop effect
- Mechanism: aspirations/expectations as reference points → higher investment
- No effects on self-efficacy, locus of control, time/risk preferences, mental health
- Full exercise script in Appendix C.2 — our script adapted from this

### McKenzie, Mohpal & Yang (2021) — "Aspirations and Financial Decisions" (Philippines)
- RCT with 2,400 microfinance clients, 8-session "dream big" aspirations treatment
- Participants achieved only 5% of savings goals
- **15% reduction in borrowing, 37% reduction in business investment** two years later
- Mechanism: frustration → reduced internal locus of control
- **Critical for our design**: script must frame goals as attainable, not ambitious. Feasibility check in Step 2 directly addresses this

### Macours & Vakis (2016) — "Sustaining Impacts" (Nicaragua CCT)
- Exposure to successful female leaders → sustained higher investments in children 2 years post-program
- Mechanism: social interaction with visible local success stories
- Less directly applicable (community-level, year-long exposure vs. our 10-min visit)

### Beaman, Duflo, Pande & Topalova (2012) — "Female Leadership Raises Aspirations" (India, *Science*)
- Female leadership quotas → gender gap in aspirations closed 25–32%, educational attainment gap erased
- Mechanism: role model effect (no change in labor market or infrastructure)
- Requires sustained exposure — supports broader theory, less applicable to one-shot visit

### Macours & Vakis (2009) — Working paper version of 2016 paper
- Same findings measured during intervention rather than 2 years after

## Known risks (to pre-register)

1. **Crowding-out (Orkin et al. 2023)**: Cash alone raises aspirations. 2b is delivered after the token and before the LLS — recipients already know $594 is coming. The cash may already activate the aspiration-raising mechanism. A null result on 2b vs. 2c is consistent with this and should not be interpreted as "planning doesn't work" — only that it doesn't add to what cash already does in this context.

2. **Frustration (McKenzie et al. 2021)**: If recipients set goals they cannot achieve, the exercise could backfire. Script includes feasibility check in Step 2 and FO training explicitly warns against "dream big" framing. Risk remains if FOs do not follow protocol.

3. **Low dosage**: 10 min, no video, no group, no follow-up = ~1/8 of Orkin et al.'s 80-min workshop. Effect sizes, if any, will be smaller. Q3 is already likely underpowered (MDE ~0.28 SD, labeling/messaging effects typically 0.10–0.25 SD).
