# Adaptation Aspirations Planning — Field Officer Training Guide

**Program**: QCF Uganda Cash for Climate Adaptation
**Audience**: Field officers delivering sub-arm 2b visits
**Training duration**: ~90 minutes
**Trainer**: Research team lead

---

## Training Agenda

| Time | Activity | Duration |
|------|----------|----------|
| 0:00 | Welcome and overview | 10 min |
| 0:10 | Walk through the script | 15 min |
| 0:25 | Common mistakes and how to avoid them | 15 min |
| 0:40 | Practice round 1 (pairs) | 15 min |
| 0:55 | Debrief and feedback | 10 min |
| 1:05 | Practice round 2 (pairs, switch roles) | 15 min |
| 1:20 | Final Q&A and logistics | 10 min |

## 1. Welcome and Overview (10 min)

**What to tell FOs:**

> "You will be visiting certain households in Group 2 villages to run a short planning exercise — about 10 minutes per household. The purpose is to help the recipient think about how they could use their cash transfer to prepare for climate risks. You are not giving advice. You are helping them think through their own plan."

**Key points to cover:**

- This visit happens after the recipient has received the $50 token and before the $594 lump sum
- Not every household in Group 2 gets this visit — only those on your task list
- The recipient should not know that other households are doing something different
- You will record the recipient's answers for research purposes
- The exercise should feel like a conversation, not a survey

**Do NOT explain:**
- The research design (three sub-arms, randomization)
- That some households receive SMS messages instead
- That some households receive nothing extra

---

## 2. Walk Through the Script (15 min)

Read through the full script (`aap_field_script.md`) aloud with the group. At each step, pause and explain:

### Step 1: Climate Best Possible Self
- **Purpose**: Get them thinking about what "prepared for climate risks" looks like for their household
- **Your role**: Ask the question, listen, probe for details
- **Common issue**: Recipient gives a one-word answer ("good"). Use the follow-up probes: "What would your farm look like? What about your home?"
- **Do not**: Suggest examples yourself. Let them generate their own vision

### Step 2: Goal Selection
- **Purpose**: Narrow from a vision to one concrete, achievable goal
- **Your role**: Repeat back what they said, then ask them to pick one
- **Critical point**: The goal must be something they believe they can *actually do*. This is the most important part of the exercise. If they pick something very large — like "build a new house" or "buy a truck" — gently ask: "Do you think you could achieve that with your transfer and what you already have? If not, is there a smaller step you could start with?"
- **Why this matters**: Research shows that when people set goals that are too ambitious and then fail to reach them, they become discouraged and invest *less* than if they had never set a goal at all. We want realistic, achievable goals — not big dreams.

### Step 3: First Step
- **Purpose**: Turn the goal into an immediate action
- **Your role**: Push for specifics. "What would you do?" is not enough. Ask: "Who would you talk to? What would you buy? Where would you go? When — this week?"
- **Common issue**: Recipient says something vague like "I would plan." Push gently: "What does planning look like? What is the very first thing you would do?"

### Step 4: Obstacles
- **Purpose**: Help them anticipate what could go wrong and think of a backup
- **Your role**: Ask both questions — the obstacle AND the backup plan
- **Common issue**: Recipient says "nothing could go wrong" or "God will provide." Acknowledge respectfully, then try: "Sometimes unexpected things happen — heavy rains, a family emergency, prices going up. If something like that happened, what would you do?"

### Step 5: Motivation
- **Purpose**: Connect the goal to how their life would improve — this makes the plan feel real and worth pursuing
- **Your role**: Ask, listen, affirm. This is the easiest step.

### Closing
- Write their goal and first step on the Planning Sheet
- Ask them where they will put it — get a specific answer ("on the wall by the door")
- Thank them and leave

## 3. Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them (15 min)

Read each mistake aloud. Ask FOs: "Why is this a problem?"

### Mistake 1: Suggesting answers
> ❌ "You could try planting sorghum — it is more drought-resistant."
> ✅ "What kind of changes to your farming could help you deal with drought?"

**Why it matters**: If you suggest the answer, we are measuring your ideas, not theirs. The whole point is to see what *they* come up with.

### Mistake 2: Encouraging big dreams
> ❌ "Think big! What is your biggest dream for your family?"
> ✅ "Which goal is most important to you and something you believe you could realistically achieve?"

**Why it matters**: Research from the Philippines showed that when people were encouraged to set very ambitious goals, they failed to reach them and then gave up — they actually invested *less* money than people who were never asked to set goals. We want achievable plans, not big dreams.

### Mistake 3: Rushing through the exercise
> ❌ Reading all five steps in 3 minutes without pausing for answers.
> ✅ Asking each question, waiting for a full answer, probing if needed.

**Why it matters**: If the recipient does not actually think through their plan, the exercise has no effect. Silence is fine — give them time to think.

### Mistake 4: Turning it into a survey
> ❌ Reading questions in a flat voice, writing answers without eye contact.
> ✅ Having a conversation. Making eye contact. Responding to what they say before moving to the next question.

**Why it matters**: This is a planning exercise, not a questionnaire. The recipient should feel like you are genuinely interested in their plan.

### Mistake 5: Sharing information about the research
> ❌ "Some households get SMS messages and some get this visit."
> ✅ Say nothing about other households or the research design.

**Why it matters**: If recipients know they are in a study, it changes how they answer. Keep it simple: "I am here to help you think through a plan."

### Mistake 6: Skipping the Planning Sheet
> ❌ Finishing the conversation and leaving without writing anything down.
> ✅ Always write the goal and first step on the sheet and hand it to them.

**Why it matters**: The sheet is the only thing that stays after you leave. It is the reminder. Without it, the conversation is forgotten by tomorrow.

## 4. Practice Round 1 (15 min)

Split into pairs. One person is the FO, the other plays the recipient.

**Recipient instructions** (give to the person playing the recipient):
> You are a farmer in Bulambuli. You grow maize and beans. Last year, heavy rains flooded your field and you lost most of your maize. You have a small house with a grass roof. You have three children in school. You just received a $50 token transfer and know that $594 is coming. You are worried about flooding again this year.

**FO instructions**: Run the full script. The trainer observes and takes notes.

---

## 5. Debrief (10 min)

After Practice Round 1, discuss as a group:

- Did the FO suggest answers or stay neutral?
- Did the recipient pick a realistic goal? If not, did the FO redirect?
- Did the FO probe for specifics in Step 3?
- Did the FO write the Planning Sheet and ask where to put it?
- How long did it take? (Target: 8–12 minutes)

---

## 6. Practice Round 2 (15 min)

Switch roles. New recipient scenario:

**Recipient instructions**:
> You are a farmer in Amuria. You grow sorghum and keep a few goats. The last two years have been very dry and your harvests have been poor. You have been thinking about starting a small shop but you do not know how. You have one child who is not in school because you cannot afford fees. You just received the $50 token.

Run the full script again. Trainer observes.

---

## 7. Final Q&A and Logistics (10 min)

Cover:

- **Task list**: You will receive a list of households to visit. Visit only those households. Do not visit households not on your list.
- **Timing**: Visit after the token is confirmed delivered. Do not visit before the token.
- **Materials**: Bring the script, a pen, and blank Planning Sheets to every visit.
- **Recording**: Fill in all data fields after each visit. Do not leave blanks — if the recipient did not answer a question, write "no answer."
- **Problems**: If the recipient is not home, try once more on a different day. If they refuse, record "refused" and move on. Do not pressure anyone to participate.
- **Questions**: Ask now. Once you are in the field, call [supervisor name/number] if something comes up that is not covered here.
